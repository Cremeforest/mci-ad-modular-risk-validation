I recently completed a longitudinal clinical-AI project for MCI-to-Alzheimer's disease conversion prediction under incomplete clinical assessment. The final model is a summary-augmented feature-level modular longitudinal model: each clinical feature module combines observed-visit temporal encoding with local trajectory summaries, and the model fuses available modules for 1/2/3/5-year risk prediction. After internal development and calibration auditing, I externally validated the frozen ADNI-trained model on a NACC first-MCI cohort under a no-ADAS13 setting without retraining. The model retained moderate external discrimination across horizons, while raw absolute risks showed cohort-shift underestimation; cross-fitted local Platt recalibration substantially improved calibration without changing the prediction model. I frame the project as a retrospective, auditable clinical-AI modeling study rather than a deployment-ready system.
